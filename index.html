⚡ Quick Hits — Trend Basics
Price makes Higher Highs + Higher Lows = Uptrend. Lower Highs + Lower Lows = Downtrend.
NEVER trade against the trend on a 5-min chart unless you have strong multi-timeframe confluence.
Confirm trend with EMAs: price above 8/21/50 EMA stacked = strong uptrend.
Volume confirms trend: rising price + rising volume = healthy trend.
Wait for pullback to EMAs or VWAP — don't chase entries at the top of a move.
⚡ Quick Hits — VWAP
VWAP = Volume Weighted Average Price — the fair value of the stock intraday.
Institutions buy near VWAP. Price above VWAP = bullish bias. Below = bearish.
VWAP reclaim (price crosses from below to above) is a powerful long entry signal.
VWAP rejection (price fails to hold above) is a short entry signal.
First VWAP test of the day after a strong open = highest-probability pullback entry.
⚡ Quick Hits — MA Pullback
8 EMA = fastest, most aggressive entries. 21 EMA = balanced. 50 EMA = slower, stronger support.
Best pullbacks touch the EMA with decreasing volume, then bounce with a green candle.
EMAs must be stacked (8 above 21 above 50) for a valid trend pullback long.
Stop goes just below the EMA that price bounced from — no exceptions.
Do not take an MA pullback trade if EMAs are flat or tangled — no clear trend.
⚡ Quick Hits — Entry & Exit Rules
Entry = first green candle that closes above the EMA after pullback + volume confirmation.
Stop loss = below the low of the entry candle OR below the EMA — whichever is tighter.
Minimum R:R = 2:1. Risk $1 to make $2. Never take a trade below 1.5:1 R:R.
Scale out: take 50% at 1R, move stop to breakeven, let rest run to 2R–3R.
If price closes below VWAP after entry, exit immediately — setup is invalid.
⚡ Quick Hits — Risk Management
Risk no more than 1–2% of account per trade. On $10K account = max $100–$200 risk per trade.
Max daily loss = 3% of account. Hit that limit? Stop trading for the day. No exceptions.
Position size = (Account × Risk%) ÷ (Entry − Stop). Calculate before every single trade.
Two losing trades in a row? Step away for 15 minutes before the next trade.
Protect capital first. A good day trading is surviving to trade another day.
⚡ Quick Hits — Candlesticks
Hammer at EMA support = bullish reversal signal. Must have long lower wick, small body.
Bullish engulfing at VWAP = strong buy signal. Confirms institutional buying.
Doji at resistance = indecision. Wait for next candle direction before entering.
Large red candle breaking below EMA with volume = get out or don't enter.
Inside bar at EMA = coiled spring setup. Break above inside bar high = long entry.
⚡ Quick Hits — ETF Playbook
SPY = S&P 500. Most liquid ETF. Tight spreads. Best for all setups. Start here.
QQQ = Nasdaq 100. More volatile than SPY. Better for momentum setups.
TQQQ/SOXL = 3x leveraged. Extreme moves. Advanced only — use smaller size.
IWM = small caps. More volatile, wider spreads. Watch SPY trend for direction clue.
Trade SPY or QQQ until consistently profitable. Leverage kills beginner accounts.
⚡ Quick Hits — Market Timing
Best trading windows: 9:30–11:30 AM ET (open) and 3:00–4:00 PM ET (power hour).
11:30 AM–2:00 PM ET = lunch chop. Low volume, choppy action. Sit out or use smaller size.
Pre-market gaps above prior high = bullish open. Plan long setups on first pullback to VWAP.
Red pre-market + opens below prior day low = bearish bias. No long setups until VWAP reclaim.
9:30–9:45 AM = "Wild West." Let price find direction before taking your first trade.
⚡ Quick Hits — Trading Psychology
FOMO = Fear Of Missing Out. You WILL miss trades. That is fine. Protect capital over chasing.
Revenge trading after a loss = fastest way to blow an account. Walk away. Come back tomorrow.
Journal every trade: entry, stop, target, actual outcome, what you did right/wrong.
The goal is not to be right. The goal is to follow the process and manage risk.
Consistency over big wins. 60% win rate + 2:1 R:R = very profitable over time.

Position Size Formula
📐 Formula
Shares = (Account Size × Risk %) ÷ (Entry Price − Stop Price)
Example: $10,000 account × 1% risk = $100 risk. Entry $450, Stop $448.50 (gap = $1.50). Shares = $100 ÷ $1.50 = 66 shares.
